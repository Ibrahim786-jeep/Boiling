Introduction

  • Nucleate boiling is an efficient mode of heat transfer where vapor bubbles form and detach from a heated surface. Its performance depends heavily on surface characteristics such as roughness, wettability, and geometry.
  • Inspired by natural surfaces (lotus leaf, shark skin, leaf veins), this study examines how bio-inspired micro-patterns enhance boiling heat transfer, focusing on bubble dynamics, heat transfer coefficient (HTC), and critical heat flux (CHF).

Key Insights:

  • Integration of ML/data-driven models with classical boiling physics is an emerging trend.
  • Thin surface geometries, wettability, roughness, and microstructure all influence bubble dynamics and heat transfer.
  • Existing correlations often lack generality; models linking geometry to bubble behavior are needed.

Research Gaps & Problem Statement

  • Gap 1: No systematic link between bio-inspired pattern geometry and boiling parameters such as bubble frequency, HTC, CHF, bubble dynamics, surface temperature (thermocouples), visualization of nucleation site density.
  • Gap 2: Lack of research on nucleate boiling behavior over bio-inspired microstructured surfaces, leaving potential heat transfer enhancement mechanisms unexplored.
  • Gap 3: Benchmark the bio-inspired surface against existing coated surfaces to evaluate nucleate boiling enhancement.
  • Gap 4: Limited understanding of bio-inspired microstructured surfaces' real-life performance.
  • Problem Statement: Conventional surfaces limit bubble departure and rewetting, causing premature transition to film boiling.

Research Objectives

  1. Design and fabricate bio-inspired micro-patterns (lotus, shark, leaf geometries).
  2. Study boiling heat transfer characteristics on each surface.
  3. Measure bubble site density, departure diameter, HTC, bubble dynamics (high-speed camera), and surface temperature (thermocouples).
  4. Develop correlations linking pattern geometry to performance.
  5. Benchmark against smooth and coated surfaces.

Methodology

  • Fabrication: Micro-machining of bio-inspired geometries.
  • Characterization: Contact angle and roughness measurement.
  • Pool Boiling Test: Controlled heat flux (50–200 kW/m²) using distilled water.
  • Data: Surface temperature, heat flux, bubble dynamics (high-speed camera).
  • Analysis: Derive HTC, CHF, bubble frequency; compare with other coated surfaces.

Significance

Significance: Bio-inspired surface micro-patterns enhance boiling by increasing nucleation site density, improving liquid rewetting, and reducing surface superheat resulting in higher Heat Transfer Coefficient (HTC) and Critical Heat Flux (CHF), thus improving energy efficiency and safety.

Areas of Application:

  • Electronics cooling and data centers for efficient heat removal.
  • Power generation and nuclear reactors for high heat dissipation.
  • Heat exchangers, HVAC, and desalination systems for optimized thermal management.
  • Aerospace and automotive systems requiring compact, high-performance cooling.

Need of Research: Conventional smooth or coated surfaces have limited nucleation sites and poor rewetting resulting in early vapor film formation. Bio-inspired patterned surfaces mimic natural geometries enhancing capillary action, liquid supply, and vapor removal. Quantifying pattern geometry effect on bubble dynamics, HTC, and CHF is crucial for next-generation boiling surfaces.
